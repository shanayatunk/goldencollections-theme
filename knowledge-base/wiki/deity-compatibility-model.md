# Deity Jewellery Compatibility Model

Backlinks: [[business-entity.md]], [[search-entity-map.md]], [[content-roadmap.md]]

## Purpose

Golden Collections deity jewellery should be mapped by three axes:

1. Deity or deity group.
2. Idol size and measured ornament dimensions.
3. Ornament/accessory type.

This is important because some deity items are deity-specific, some fit multiple deities, and some are common/general alankaram accessories.

## Size Rules

Every deity jewellery product should eventually expose:

- Product measurement: height, width, depth/arc where relevant.
- Idol fit note: suggested idol height range.
- Placement: head, forehead, ears, chest, neck, waist, hands, feet, back/arch, peedam/base.
- Measurement confidence: measured from product variant, measuring-tape image, owner supplied, or inferred.
- Fit caveat: idol posture, face width, crown style, and draping can affect fit.

## Compatibility Classes

- `deity_specific`: strongly associated with one deity/group.
- `multi_deity`: works for a defined set of deities.
- `general_common`: generally usable across many god/goddess idols if size fits. Use this when compatibility is not perfectly established but the ornament is broadly usable.
- `festival_specific`: tied to a festival/ritual season such as Varalakshmi Vratham.

## Deity Groups

### Varalakshmi / Lakshmi / Amman

Priority: first.

Common product families:

- Varalakshmi idol/doll.
- Varalakshmi face.
- Hastham and padam / hands and legs / ashtapatham.
- Crown/mukut/kireedam.
- Hair crown.
- Short haram.
- Long haram.
- Vaddanam/waist belt.
- Earrings.
- Nose ring/nath/bullaku.
- Pendants/pathakam.
- Vagamalai/thomala/bhujalu.
- Arch/prabhavali.
- Banana tree, lotus asana, coconut stand, kalasam/sanctum decor.

Compatibility notes:

- Varalakshmi products are often festival-specific for May-August search demand.
- Lakshmi/Amman-style ornaments may overlap with Durga/Devi/Parvati depending on design and size.
- Hands/legs and faces are not universal; they should map to Varalakshmi/Lakshmi/Amman doll/idol use cases.

### Vishnu / Balaji / Venkateswara / Perumal

Common product families:

- Namam / thiruman / Vaishnav tilak.
- Shanku chakra / shankh chakra.
- Crown/mukut/kireedam.
- Long haram and short haram.
- Pendants/pathakam.
- Vaddanam/waist belt.
- Sun and moon ornaments where suitable.

Compatibility notes:

- Namam/thiruman and shanku chakra should be treated as Vishnu/Balaji/Perumal-specific or Vishnu-family, not universal.
- Balaji/Venkateswara products should include aliases like Srinivasa, Tirupati Balaji, Venkatachalapati, and Perumal where relevant.

### Krishna / Radha Krishna

Common product families:

- Crown/mukut.
- Peacock/feather-themed crown where available.
- Necklace/haram.
- Waist belt.
- Earrings.
- Flute-related accessory if available.
- Radha-Krishna paired alankaram where relevant.

Compatibility notes:

- Krishna products can overlap with Vishnu-family jewellery but should support Krishna-specific naming where motifs fit.

### Ganesha / Ganapati / Vinayaka

Common product families:

- Crown/mukut.
- Necklace/haram.
- Earrings.
- Pendant/pathakam.
- Waist belt where idol shape allows.
- Tilak/bindi where suitable.

Compatibility notes:

- Fit can be affected by trunk direction, head width, and seated posture.

### Shiva / Mahadev / Shankara

Common product families:

- Tripund / vibhuti / thiruneer tilak.
- Crown or hair accessory where appropriate.
- Rudraksha-style necklace if available.
- Trishul/weapon accessories.
- Snake/naga motifs if available.

Compatibility notes:

- Tripund/vibhuti marks should be Shiva-specific, not universal.
- Shiva linga products need separate handling from anthropomorphic Shiva idols.

### Durga / Devi / Amman / Parvati

Common product families:

- Crown/mukut/kireedam.
- Nose ring/nath/bullaku.
- Earrings.
- Short and long harams.
- Vaddanam/waist belt.
- Weapons such as trishul, sword, chakra, conch where relevant to the form.
- Arch/prabhavali.

Compatibility notes:

- Amman, Durga, Parvati, Lakshmi, and Devi terms can overlap in customer search, but product fit and deity-specific weapons/symbols should be kept explicit.

### Murugan / Subramanya / Kartikeya / Skanda

Common product families:

- Crown/mukut/kireedam.
- Vel/spear weapon accessory if available.
- Necklace/haram.
- Waist belt.
- Earrings.

Compatibility notes:

- Use aliases Murugan, Subramanya, Kartikeya, Skanda, Shanmukha where relevant.

### Ayyappa / Ayyappan / Dharma Shastha

Common product families:

- Crown.
- Haram/necklace.
- Waist belt.
- Tilak/forehead mark where relevant.
- General alankaram accessories by size.

Compatibility notes:

- Ayyappa fit is posture-sensitive because many idols are seated in yogic posture.

### Hanuman / Anjaneya

Common product families:

- Crown/mukut.
- Necklace/haram.
- Mace/gada accessory if available.
- Waist belt.
- Tilak where relevant.

Compatibility notes:

- Hanuman products should support aliases Anjaneya, Maruti, and Bajrangbali.

## Common Alankaram Accessories

These may be common across many deities if dimensions fit:

- Generic crown/mukut/kireedam.
- Generic short haram.
- Generic long haram.
- Generic earrings.
- Generic vaddanam/waist belt.
- Generic pendant/pathakam.
- Arch/prabhavali.
- Peedam/base/lotus asana.
- Flower garlands and thomala where size fits.

## Deity-First Shopping Structure

Golden Collections should eventually support deity-based collections so customers can browse by the deity first, then by ornament type.

Example:

- Amman Jewellery
- Amman Crown / Mukut
- Amman Necklace / Haram
- Amman Earrings
- Amman Vaddanam / Waist Belt
- Amman Nose Ring
- Amman Hands and Legs
- Amman Accessories

This is especially useful for customers who do not start with product terminology. A customer may search `Amman jewellery` before knowing whether they need a crown, haram, vaddanam, or earrings.

## Collection Template Strategy

Owner-confirmed decision: do not mix the current ornament/category collections with a global "shop this deity by ornament" navigation strip.

### Current Deity Collections

Existing deity collections, such as Varalakshmi collections, deity crowns, deity necklaces, deity earrings, vaddanam, decorative items, and deity accessories, should use one clean deity collection template.

For these pages:

- Keep the hero simple: breadcrumb, H1, short buying intro, product count, and trust/fit signals.
- Do not show a duplicate top navigation like `Shop this deity by ornament` when the page already has context-specific subcollection circles below.
- Let Shopify category node references and subcollection circles drive the page-specific shopping path.
- Keep customer focus on fast product discovery, size confidence, and fewer pre-purchase questions.
- Keep SEO/GEO content useful but not visually confusing.

### Future Shop By Deity Collections

Create a separate future shopping path for deity-first browsing.

Example:

1. `Shop by Deity`
2. `Amman`
3. `Amman Crowns`, `Amman Necklaces`, `Amman Earrings`, `Amman Vaddanam`, `Amman Accessories`

These deity-first pages should show products that are:

- Specifically tagged or metafielded for that deity.
- Marked `General/Common` when the product can work across many deities and the size/placement fits.

This may require a separate collection template from the current deity category template. The deity-first template should use deity compatibility data, ornament type data, and size fields to build tighter subcollection groups and reduce customer confusion.

End goal: help customers shop faster, understand fit more easily, reduce WhatsApp sizing queries, and make the catalog clearer for Google and answer engines in 2026.

## Deity-Specific Or Semi-Specific Accessories

- Namam/thiruman: Vishnu, Balaji, Venkateswara, Perumal.
- Shanku chakra: Vishnu, Balaji, Venkateswara, Perumal.
- Tripund/vibhuti/thiruneer: Shiva.
- Trishul: Shiva, Durga, Amman, Parvati forms where appropriate.
- Vel: Murugan/Subramanya.
- Mustache: deity/form-specific and should not be treated as universal.
- Hastham/padam: Varalakshmi/Lakshmi/Amman doll/idol context.
